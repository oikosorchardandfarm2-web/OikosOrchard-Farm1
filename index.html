Fresh & Organic Farming

Nestled in the highlands of South Cebu, Oikos Orchard and Farm sits 300 masl, offering breathtaking views of Bohol Island. It is a living testament to sustainable agriculture, biodiversity, and mindful stewardship of the land.

About Our Oikos Orchard & Farm

Oikos Orchard and Farm is an agricultural tourist destination located at Vegetable Highway, Upper Bae, Sibonga, Cebu, Philippines. The farm produces thousands of fruits and Philippine native tree seedlings of diﬀerent kinds, that were planted and grown ﬁrst hand by Oikos’ farmers in their own nursery. Apart from that, Oikos Orchard and farm, grows fruit and vegetables, raises native animals, such as goats, chickens and hogs. The farm also oﬀers glamping accommodations for nature lovers and adventurous people alike.

Oikos Orchard and Farm is a sustainable, high density, picturesque agritourism farm destination. The farm was established in 2021, by Bernard Restiﬁcar and Myrma Restiﬁcar. The farm aims to be the ﬁrst tourism park of fruit-bearing and Philippine native trees in the Philippines. Its name derives from the word, ‘Oikos’ – an ancient Greek term which refers to three related but distinct concepts namely, the family, the family’s property, and the house. This word is also infused with a great deal of meaning, hope, and promise. Oikos Orchard and Farm is an assurance of home away from home.

Our Services

Farm Tour

Experience the farm up close. Our guided farm tour offers a walk through orchards, gardens, and regenerative systems sharing stories of the land, the plants, and the people who care for them.

Car Glamping

Turn your car into your basecamp. Car Glamping is a modern camping experience where mobility meets nature, perfect for travelers, explorers, and weekend adventurers who want freedom, flexibility, and scenic nights under the stars.

Glamping

Nature, comfort, and calm glamping offers a relaxing outdoor stay with the ease of modern comforts, set in a serene natural environment.

Seedlings

These seedlings are propagated in healthy, living soil to build resilience from the start. Selected for adaptability and vitality, they support food forests, regenerative farms, and sustainable landscapes.
